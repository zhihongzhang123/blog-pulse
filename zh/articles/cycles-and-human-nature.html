Cycles and Human Nature

分类：Philosophy

Markets don't move randomly. They pulse with the collective heartbeat of human fear and greed.

The Eternal Cycle#

Hope, euphoria, anxiety, denial, panic, capitulation, despair, hope again. This cycle has repeated for centuries because human nature does not change.

"If you want to know what a man is like, give him power." — Abraham Lincoln

The same applies to markets. Give a market liquidity, and it will show you its true nature.

Why Cycles Persist#

Cycles exist because they are rooted in biology, not economics. The human brain is wired for:

  • Pattern recognition — We see trends that aren't there
  • Loss aversion — We feel losses twice as intensely as gains
  • Herd behavior — Safety in numbers, even when the herd is wrong
  • Recency bias — We extrapolate the recent past into the future

These cognitive biases create the oscillation between overvaluation and undervaluation that defines market cycles.

The Wisdom of Cycles#

Howard Marks put it best: "The pendulum swings from euphoria to despair, from overpriced to underpriced, from a belief that the future is secure to a conviction that it is bleak."

Understanding this pendulum doesn't help you predict the exact timing of its swing. But it does help you know which direction it's moving and where you are in the arc.

Conclusion#

To understand markets, understand humans. To understand humans, understand yourself. The investor who masters their own psychology has already won half the battle.
