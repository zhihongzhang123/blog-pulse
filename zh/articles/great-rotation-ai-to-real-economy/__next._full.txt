<p>For the past three years, capital has flowed relentlessly into mega-cap technology. The "Magnificent Seven" became the "Magnificent Everything." But the tide is turning — not because AI is dead, but because <strong>markets are cyclical by nature</strong>.</p>
<h2 id="the-signal-in-the-noise">The Signal in the Noise<a class="heading-anchor" href="#the-signal-in-the-noise"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>The rotation began quietly in early 2026. Industrials outperformed the S&#x26;P 500 for the first time since 2021. Energy stocks, long dismissed as dinosaurs, posted consecutive quarters of double-digit earnings growth. Infrastructure and defense contractors saw order backlogs reach all-time highs.</p>
<p>This is not a rejection of technology. It is a <strong>recognition of valuation extremes</strong>. When the top 10 stocks in the S&#x26;P 500 account for more than 35% of the index weight, mean reversion becomes not just likely — it becomes mathematically inevitable.</p>
<h2 id="what-history-teaches">What History Teaches<a class="heading-anchor" href="#what-history-teaches"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>The dot-com era offered a parallel. In 1999, technology was the future — and it was. But buying Cisco at 200x earnings was still a terrible investment, because the company went on to lose 80% of its value. The thesis was right. The price was wrong.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"Price is what you pay. Value is what you get."
— Warren Buffett</p>
</blockquote>
<h2 id="the-next-phase">The Next Phase<a class="heading-anchor" href="#the-next-phase"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>Three forces are driving this rotation:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>AI Capex Fatigue</strong> — The infrastructure buildout phase is maturing. Returns on massive GPU investments are coming under scrutiny.</li>
<li><strong>Onshoring Tailwinds</strong> — Government incentives for domestic manufacturing are translating into real capital expenditure.</li>
<li><strong>Rate Uncertainty</strong> — With the Fed signaling a data-dependent path, sectors less sensitive to discount rates (commodities, energy, industrials) are gaining favor.</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="the-investors-playbook">The Investor's Playbook<a class="heading-anchor" href="#the-investors-playbook"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>The rotation does not mean selling all tech. It means <strong>diversifying within the trend</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Reduce concentration risk in mega-cap tech</li>
<li>Add exposure to mid-cap industrials and energy</li>
<li>Maintain a core position in quality compounders</li>
<li>Keep cash reserves for dislocations</li>
</ul>
<p>The market is not telling you AI is over. It is telling you <strong>everything else has been underowned</strong>. The opportunity lies in the gap between perception and reality.</p>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion<a class="heading-anchor" href="#conclusion"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>Rotations are painful for those caught on the wrong side. But for the patient observer, they represent the most reliable source of alpha in modern markets.
