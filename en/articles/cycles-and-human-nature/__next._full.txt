<p>Markets don't move randomly. They pulse with the collective heartbeat of human fear and greed.</p>
<h2 id="the-eternal-cycle">The Eternal Cycle<a class="heading-anchor" href="#the-eternal-cycle"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>Hope, euphoria, anxiety, denial, panic, capitulation, despair, hope again. This cycle has repeated for centuries because human nature does not change.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"If you want to know what a man is like, give him power."
— Abraham Lincoln</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The same applies to markets. Give a market liquidity, and it will show you its true nature.</p>
<h2 id="why-cycles-persist">Why Cycles Persist<a class="heading-anchor" href="#why-cycles-persist"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>Cycles exist because they are rooted in biology, not economics. The human brain is wired for:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pattern recognition</strong> — We see trends that aren't there</li>
<li><strong>Loss aversion</strong> — We feel losses twice as intensely as gains</li>
<li><strong>Herd behavior</strong> — Safety in numbers, even when the herd is wrong</li>
<li><strong>Recency bias</strong> — We extrapolate the recent past into the future</li>
</ul>
<p>These cognitive biases create the oscillation between overvaluation and undervaluation that defines market cycles.</p>
<h2 id="the-wisdom-of-cycles">The Wisdom of Cycles<a class="heading-anchor" href="#the-wisdom-of-cycles"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>Howard Marks put it best: "The pendulum swings from euphoria to despair, from overpriced to underpriced, from a belief that the future is secure to a conviction that it is bleak."</p>
<p>Understanding this pendulum doesn't help you predict the exact timing of its swing. But it does help you know which direction it's moving and where you are in the arc.</p>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion<a class="heading-anchor" href="#conclusion"><span class="anchor-symbol">#</span></a></h2>
<p>To understand markets, understand humans. To understand humans, understand yourself.
